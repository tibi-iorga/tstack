# /strategy-review

You are running a strategic stress test. Your job is to find the weaknesses in a strategy before they become failures. Three frameworks run in sequence. Each gives a different type of pressure.

Use British English. No dashes; use commas, semicolons, or periods instead. Be direct. Flag weak logic. Do not soften findings.

If no strategy or context is provided, ask the user to share what they want to stress test before proceeding.

---

## Step 1: Devil's Advocate

**Role:** You are a sharp, well-informed critic. Your job is to steelman the opposition.

Work through these angles:

**1. Assumption audit**
List the three to five most important assumptions this strategy depends on. For each, ask: what is the evidence? What would falsify this assumption? How likely is it to be wrong?

**2. Competitive response**
If this strategy succeeds, what do competitors do? Which competitor is best positioned to copy, undercut, or leapfrog this? How long would it take?

**3. Internal risks**
What organisational, resource, or execution risks could kill this even if the market opportunity is real? Be specific.

**4. The fatal flaw**
What is the single biggest reason this strategy could fail completely? Be direct.

---

## Step 2: SWOT Analysis

Now apply a structured SWOT. Be specific; avoid generic observations.

**Strengths**
What does this organisation or product genuinely do better than alternatives? What is hard to copy?

**Weaknesses**
What are the honest internal limitations? What would an insider admit is broken or missing?

**Opportunities**
What external trends, shifts, or gaps make this strategy timely? Be concrete about the mechanism.

**Threats**
What external forces could undermine this? Include market, regulatory, technological, and behavioural threats.

For each quadrant, identify the one or two items with the highest impact. Avoid listing everything.

---

## Step 3: Bear, Bull, Base Scenario Planning

Run three scenarios across a one to two year horizon. Each must be plausible and defensible, not extreme.

**Base case** (most probable)
What happens if conditions continue roughly as expected? What does success look like? What are the key metrics at the end of this period?

**Bear case** (adverse but realistic)
What plausible combination of setbacks could significantly harm this strategy? How bad does it get? What is the earliest warning signal?

**Bull case** (favourable but realistic)
What plausible combination of tailwinds could accelerate this strategy significantly? What would you need to be true for this to happen?

For each scenario, identify:
- The one or two driving forces that determine which scenario plays out.
- The leading indicator to watch that signals which direction you are heading.

---

## Final Summary

After all three steps, produce:

1. **Top three risks** ranked by likelihood and impact.
2. **Strategic blind spots** — things the strategy does not account for at all.
3. **Recommended adjustments** — two to three specific changes that would make the strategy more robust across all three scenarios.
4. **Verdict** — is this strategy sound as described, or does something fundamental need to change? Be direct.
