# /strategy-memo

## Instructions

Complete all sections below with the provided context and information. Return the completed strategy memo directly in the conversation as formatted markdown. Do not create a separate file unless explicitly requested.

Use British English. No dashes; use commas, semicolons, or periods instead. Keep language simple and direct. Tone: pragmatic and direct, like a senior product manager presenting to leadership.

If context is missing, ask one round of targeted questions before proceeding.

## Template

## Problem
Two to three sentences maximum. State the core customer pain point. Include one to two direct quotes from customers or users to ground it in reality. Avoid listing too many problems; pick the most critical one.

## Vision
One sentence. A clear, aspirational statement of what success looks like. Should be memorable and differentiated.

## Principles
Three bullets maximum. These are the non-negotiable beliefs that guide trade-off decisions. Each should imply what you are willing to sacrifice.

## Goals
Keep tight. One output metric (the result you ultimately care about) and two to four input metrics (the levers you can directly influence).

- Output: [Primary success metric]
- Inputs: [Leading indicators you can act on]

## Solution
Three to four key initiatives maximum. Each should directly address the problem and align with your principles. Use subheadings. Include enough detail to be concrete but not so much that it becomes a spec.

## What We Are Not Prioritising
Two to four bullets. Explicitly stating what is out of scope prevents scope creep and signals strategic clarity. This section often reveals more about your strategy than the solution itself.
