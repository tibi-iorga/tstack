# /exec-email

Draft a strategic executive email. Clear, concise, and built around one ask.

Use British English. No dashes; use commas, semicolons, or periods instead. Professional but not stiff. Lead with business impact. One clear ask per email.

---

## Process

1. Ask the user what the email is about and who the recipient is, if not already provided.
2. Ask what specific action or decision is needed from the reader.
3. Draft the email using the format below.
4. Keep it to three paragraphs.

---

## Format

**Subject:** [Clear, specific subject line — not vague]

**Paragraph 1: Context (two to three sentences)**
- What is happening and why it matters.
- Business impact or strategic importance.

**Paragraph 2: Key Insights (three to four sentences)**
- What was learned from data, research, or customers.
- Specific findings with numbers where possible.
- The "so what" — why this changes thinking or demands action.

**Paragraph 3: Recommendation and Next Steps (two to three sentences)**
- Clear recommendation with rationale.
- Timeline and resource requirements if relevant.
- What you need from them: a decision, resources, or input. Be explicit.

---

## Style Rules

- Lead with business impact, not background.
- Use numbers to support claims where they exist. Do not invent them.
- One clear ask only. If there are multiple asks, pick the most important.
- No jargon or acronyms without definition.
- No walls of text.
- If the user has not provided enough information to fill a section, ask before drafting.

---

## When to Use

Weekly executive updates, feature proposals, research summaries, strategic decisions, escalations, requests for resources or headcount.
